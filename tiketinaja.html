    • This project is an online ticket booking application that features official ratings from IMDb and statistics from films regarding availability, number of viewers, live ratings and reviews, as well as various payment methods.


    • The Problem & The Goal

    The Problem

    A potential audience, of course, before buying a ticket, will definitely check the film rating and synopsis as well as statistics on its availability, with only one application, it makes it easy for users so they don't have to open other sites or applications that require more time. And solve the problem of payment, because there are many payment methods.

    The Goal

    Designing an application for online ticket ordering that is accompanied by an official rating feature from IMDb and statistics from films regarding availability, number of viewers, live rating, and reviews. And also a variety of payment menus so that it can make it easier for users.

    The User & Audience

    The user and audience for this mobile app are everyone and groups ranging from teenagers, adults, students, workers, etc. But the one who will interact with it the most is the people who love films.

    Role & Responsibility

    I'm a UX designer with Responsibility: Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, iterating on designs and responsive design.

    Scope & Constraints

    in this case study, i will only focus on the first version of the app. I conduct proper research of the user because user is important. I found what they need and how they would use the app.

    Process

    User Research

    I conducted a survey of film lovers to find out their habits, needs and problems when ordering tickets online. Based on survey results, most of them use general ordering applications that are not specifically for ordering tickets. This is because the application has a more attractive promo or discount and has a simple payment method. However, because this is a general application, there is no other payment menu available or there is only one payment method and there are no movie statistics.


    • Persona and User Journey

    Devie Oktarie is a college student who loves movies. as a movie buff who wants to buy tickets, he wants to order tickets with bank transfer and e-wallet payments in order to get convenience when buying tickets.


    • I created a user journey map of Devi's experience using the site to help identify possible pain points and improvement opportunities.

    • Wireframe

    The next step is to create a wireframe for the page. I created a paper wireframe first to decide and bringing together several ideas into one piece.


    • After get some idea from paper wireframe, then I created a digital wireframe for the basic overview and to serve as a reference point for mobile development.


    • Easy customization is a major requirement to pay attention to in designing.


    • Design Decisions

    Utilization of grids, icon consistency, containment, and Emphasis to create attractive visual designs.


    • Defining Colors & Design Elements

    Before heading to the mockup, i did some research what colors should i use, based on the branding and what company do. Also i compare it to the competitors. Beside colors, i also research what design elements should i use for pages, and i came up with these.


    • Mockup

    After i got the design elements, color and wireframe, i straight to the mockup design. And this is the initial design of the app.


    • Usability Study

    The Usability Study was carried out in an unmoderated manner which aimed to find out how easy the flow of ticket orders was with 5 film lovers participating. And I found this.


    • To make it more visible that the video can be played, a video play button was added to the video movie trailer, and to make it less conspicuous, the button's opacity was lowered to 50%. Also added a shadow to clarify the shape of the play button by using a drop shadow.


    • To make the cinema screen more visible and so that the audience gets an overview of the studio, the cinema screen is placed at the top, and the position of the seats is also rotated so that the audience gets a better picture.


    • Result

    With some feedback and revisions, the final design is finally done. Then the next step is the development process.
