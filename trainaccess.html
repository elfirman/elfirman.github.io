Train Access

< Back to Home

    • This project is an online train ticket booking application that has a train rating feature from the user and information on seats that have been booked, as well as various payment methods.

    The Problem & The Goal

    The Problem

    A prospective passenger, of course, before buying a ticket, will definitely check the ratings and reviews so that they are not disappointed and comfortable, with this application users can check train ratings. And also solve the problem of payment, because there are many payment methods.

    The Goal

    Designing an application for ordering train tickets online which is accompanied by a train rating feature from the user. And also various payment methods so that it can make it easier for users.

    The User & Audience

    The user and audience for this mobile app are everyone and groups ranging from teenagers, adults, students, workers, etc. But the one who will interact with it the most is the workers and students.

    Role & Responsibility

    I'm a UX designer with Responsibility: Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, iterating on designs and responsive design.

    Scope & Constraints

    in this case study, i will only focus on the first version of the app. Our mistake at the beginning is i didnâ€™t conduct proper research of the user because of a tight deadline. I only assume what they need and how they would use the app. For that reason, i heavily depend on the test result of the first mockup that i make, which i will discuss at the Process part.

    Process

    User Research

    I have conducted a survey of travelers using the train to find out their habits, needs, and problems when ordering train tickets online. Based on survey results, most of them use general ordering applications that are not specifically for ordering tickets. This is because the application has a more attractive promo or discount and has a simple payment method. However, because this is a public application, there is no other payment menu available and there are no ratings and reviews from users for trains.


    • Persona and User Journey

    Elza is a student who often travels by train who needs information about the seat she has booked because she wants to know the next seat she has booked to make her feel comfortable.


    • I created a user journey map of Elza's experience using the site to help identify possible pain points and improvement opportunities.


    • Wireframe

    The next step is to create a wireframe for the page. I created a paper wireframe first to decide and bringing together several ideas into one piece.


    • After get some idea from paper wireframe, then I created a digital wireframe for the basic overview and to serve as a reference point for mobile development.


    • Design Decisions

    Easy navigation is a major requirement that must be considered in designing. I also implement features that users want based on research.


    • Mockup

    After i got the design elements, color and wireframe, i straight to the mockup design. And this is the initial design of the app.


    • Usability Study

    I did two stages of usability studies. Findings from the first study helped guide designs from wireframes to mockups. The second study uses a high-fidelity prototype and reveals what aspects of the mockup need improvement.


    • To make it more visible and easier to press, the navigation buttons are enlarged. Also added a shadow to clarify the navigation button by using a drop shadow. and Adding the selected carriage and seat information to the payment information menu so that users can check again if there is an mistake or not.


    • Result

    With some feedback and revisions, the final design is finally done. Then the next step is the development process.
