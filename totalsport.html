Total Sport

< Back to Home

    • A Responsive web for online field bookings and can easily be via phone, laptop, tablet, computer and all kinds of electronics that have field rating features from users and, as well as various payment methods.

    The Problem & The Goal

    The Problem

    Available online booking websites have cluttered designs, inefficient systems for browsing through products, and confusing checkout processes.

    The Goal

    1. A responsive website with Design to be user friendly by providing clear navigation and offering a fast checkout process.
    2. Rent a sports field only by website, can use laptops, desktops, smartphones, tablets, even smartTVs


    • The User & Audience

    The target audience is people with a passion for sports who frequently rent fields and don't have the extra time to rent offline.

    Role & Responsibility

    I'm a UX designer with Responsibility: Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, iterating on designs and responsive design.

    Scope & Constraints

    This is TotalSport first website, so i create the site from scratch. From the look and feel, design elements, and the design of each page. These are the pages :
    1. Homepage
    2. Search page
    3. Fields page
    4. Detail page
    5. Checkout page
    6. About page
    7. Contact page

    Process

    User Research

    I conducted user interviews, which I then turned into empathy maps to better understand the target user and their needs. I found that many target users want to easily rent a field by simply going through the internet. Many users don't have time to come to the field just to booking (offline booking).


    • Persona and User Journey

    Fasya is a person who likes sports who needs online field booking because he don’t have time to booking offline.


    • I created a user journey map of Fasya’s experience using the site to help identify possible pain points and improvement opportunities.


    • Sitemap

    the first step that i took when i have a deal was creating a sitemap so everyone can see the whole structure of the website. This sitemap is very useful to see how big the scope is and also as my guide when designing the pages.


    • Wireframe

    The next step is to create a wireframe for the page. I created a paper wireframe first. Then I created a digital wireframe for the basic overview and to serve as a reference point for site development. because this is a responsive website and can adjust the screen according to the device used, so I made several screen variations: desktop, tablet and smartphone versions.


    • Defining Colors and Design Elements

    Before heading to the mockup, i did some research what colors should i use, based on the branding and what company do. Also i compare it to the competitors. Beside colors, i also research what design elements should i use for pages.

    Mockup

    After i got the design elements and color, i straight to the mockup design. And this is the initial design of the original size.


    • I included considerations for additional screen sizes in my mockups based on my earlier wireframes. Because users shop from a variety of devices, I felt it was important to optimize the browsing experience for a range of device sizes, such as mobile and tablet so users have the smoothest experience possible.


    • Usability study

    Based on the insights from the usability study, I made changes to improve the option menu on detail page, so user can choose date and time.


    • To make easier use and simple, I added a rating on search results.


    • Result

    With some feedback and revisions, the final design is finally done. Then the next step is the development process.
